Realism

Skull of a Skeleton with Burning Cigarette by Vincent van Gogh

Realism primarily focuses on creating a realistic and accurate depiction of objects or events. This type of art rejects imaginative features in favor of more detailed physical appearances.
(Britannica, 2022)

History of the Art:

Realism became a style during the mid-19th century in France. Widely present in French novels and paintings during the 1850s, the word “realism” itself was first used to describe a doctrine focused on summarizing what it offered to the writer in his time. Thus, realism not only refers to the real life appearance but the essence of the contemporary as well.

Gustave Courbet was one of, if not, the first artist to push for the realism aesthetic. One of his works, “The Painter’s Studio” (top), along with other pieces, were put under a label he called “Realism, G. Courbet”. Another one of his famous paintings, “A Burial at Ornans” (left), struck the audience for showing peasants and labor workers as they are, truly reflecting the state of his period. (Britannica, 2022)

Some Styling Tips:

1.) Have a variety of brushes / pencils.
Realism relies on the fact that they look similar to real life and not stylized. Having different types of drawing materials for specific occasions can effectively bring the details out better.

2.) Sketch out the illustration first or have a draft.
No one wants to deal with a drawing with no direction, or commit far into it only to realize you created a large inaccuracy at the beginning. To save yourself from some pain, having an effective layout drafted first enables you to plan and visualize the final product better.

3.) Transition elements smoothly.
In real life, we will not find outlines in everyday objects to express shape usually. The visual differences are that distinguishes them. This can range from gradual light levelling, avoiding sharp edges unless necessary, and the color values.

4.) Know the color aesthetic beforehand.
Most often, realism images consists of many different shades of color, all with harmony with each other. Choosing what colors to blend with another wisely while sticking to a theme can bring out another layer of reality in the piece.
